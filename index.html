How to Start
01
Pick a goal, not a technology
One outcome — equity gap reduction, educator pipeline, federal compliance velocity. Not three. The goal filter shows the agents that actually move that needle.
02
Build the floor before the ceiling
Period 6–7 agents only work when the Lanthanide backbone (data integration, rules engines, SIS/LMS connectors) is solid. Underfund that and everything above gets brittle fast.
03
Governance runs in parallel
The Actinide trust layer isn't a phase-3 deliverable. FERPA, Title VI civil rights compliance, and equity impact assessment must stand up alongside the first transactional agent that touches student data.
04
Pilot in 30–60 days
Real users, real data, narrow scope. A Period 2 informational agent (Policy Q&A, Licensure Status) carries low risk and ships fast — it builds organizational confidence for harder agents.
05
Measure one thing
Equity gap close rate, licensure cycle time, IDEA due-process resolution speed, federal drawdown accuracy. Pick one metric per agent. If it moves, scale. If not, understand why.
06
Talk in symbols, not slideware
The two-letter codes (Ew, Lc, Fg) become shared vocabulary across CIO, Chief Academic Officer, General Counsel, and legislature. "Fg needs to clear before Ia ships" beats a 40-page deck.
